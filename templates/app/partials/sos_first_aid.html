  1. Move away from the snake — do not attempt to catch or kill it.
  2. Keep the victim calm and still — movement spreads venom faster.
  3. Immobilise the bitten limb below heart level.
  4. Remove tight items (rings, watches, bracelets) near the bite.
  5. Do NOT cut the bite, suck out venom, or apply a tourniquet.
  6. Note the time of bite and describe the snake to emergency services.
  7. Get to hospital immediately — call 10177 (ambulance).

  2. Immobilise the limb using a makeshift splint (sticks, rolled clothing).
  3. Pad around the break with soft material for comfort.
  4. Elevate the limb if possible to reduce swelling.
  5. Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth for up to 20 minutes.
  6. For open fracture (bone visible): cover with clean cloth, do not push bone back.
  7. Call 10177 for an ambulance or transport to nearest hospital.

  1. Lay the person flat on their back.

  5. Do not give anything by mouth until fully conscious.

  1. Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for minimum 20 minutes.
  2. Remove jewellery/clothing near the burn — unless stuck to skin.
  3. Do NOT apply butter, toothpaste, ice, or home remedies.
  4. Cover loosely with clean cling film or non-fluffy material.
